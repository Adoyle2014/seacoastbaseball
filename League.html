Seacoast NH Men's Senior League

Rules & Regulations

This league was created to provide for men 28 years old and over an alternative to softball and enable us to play the game we all grew up playing. Adult attitudes must always prevail, no matter how competitive any league is. The responsibility that this ethic sustains will remain at the responsibility of the individual league President, anyone who abuses the league’s code and or acts against the President can be suspended or expelled from the league.

  1. UNIFORMS, HELMETS AND BASEBALLS

      1. All teams must have full baseball uniforms consisting of caps, baseball jerseys and baseball pants, each player’s uniform must be of similar design to his teammates uniforms. Teams having sponsorship will be allowed the sponsors name on their uniforms via patch or uniform shirt.
      2. No player out of uniform will be allowed to play without the opposing manager’s consent.
      3. All teams should have their uniforms by their first game. A grace period should be extended for newly activated players or for new teams to complete their uniforms.
      1. All batters and runners must wear helmets for both at bats and on base. There is no option to this rule. Full double-ear flap helmets are encouraged for at bats and are encouraged for the bases. Catchers must wear a helmet underneath their mask. All base coaches are also required to wear protective head gear while on the field of play. The same rule will apply for all national tournament play.
      2. The Seacoast NH MSBL will use official Rawlings MSBL baseballs: RMSBL. Each game should have a minimum of 6 balls to each game. Only balls provided by the Seacoast NH MSBL will be permitted.
      3. Wood bats only are allowed and will be permitted for all tournaments. No metal bats If a player uses a bat that is not a wood bat and a protest is made, the offending batter will be declared an out.
      4. Metal cleats are permitted.

  2. GAME LENGTH, RUN RULE AND RAIN-OUTS

      1. All Regular Season Games are 9 innings - Re: darkness - 7 innings complete – umpires discretion Re: rain - 5 innings complete. The umpire may suspend, cancel or call a game if, in their opinion, the safety of the players is compromised due to rain, darkness or time limits.All Double-Headers shall be 7 inning games.

        Playoffs

        All league playoff games must be played to completion, (9) inning game. In the event of rain or darkness, the game will be suspended at that point and resumed at a later date until all 9 innings have been completed.

      2. If there is a 15-run discrepancy at the end of 7 innings, the game will be ruled final at this point. This rule will not pertain in national and regional tournaments.
      3. Any games rained out will not be re-scheduled by the league in coordination with the Umpires and Teams involved. Rain out games must be made up in order of cancellations. Tie games can be played to completion at a later date, again in coordination with the Umpires and Teams involved. Final league standings will be determined by won/lost percentage. Ties will be decided by head-to-head competition first, followed by inter-division won-lost record.
      4. In the event of rain, it is the responsibility of the President to notify the umpires and the teams of field conditions and possible cancellation of game, at least 2 hours before scheduled game time. In the event such notification does not take place, the Umpires will be responsible for making a call at the field.
      5. In the event a team needs to reschedule a game the opposing Manager is required to notify both the home Manager and League Commissioner 24-hours in advance.
